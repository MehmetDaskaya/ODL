Password Security

Welcome to Password Security Basics

In today's digital world, a strong password is your first line of defense against cyber threats. This interactive module will guide you through creating passwords that are both secure and memorable.

Did you know? Over 80% of data breaches are caused by weak or stolen passwords.

Why Password Security Matters

Identity Theft

Weak passwords can lead to your personal information being stolen, resulting in financial loss and damage to your reputation.

Financial Loss

Hackers can gain access to your bank accounts, credit cards, and other financial services through compromised passwords.

Social Engineering

Access to one account can lead to compromised security across multiple platforms due to password reuse.

Characteristics of a Strong Password

A strong password is the foundation of your digital security. Let's explore what makes a password strong and how to avoid common pitfalls.

Password Strength Factors

Length

At least 12 characters long. Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack.

Complexity

Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters (e.g., !@#$%)

Uniqueness

Different for each account and not based on obvious personal information

Weak vs. Strong Passwords

Weak Password Examples Why It's Weak Strong Alternative
password123 Common word with predictable numbers P@$$w0rd-123-XyZ!
qwerty Keyboard pattern, extremely common Qw3r+y-7p0!nTs
john1990 Name with birth year, personal info J0hn*19-90!pLuS
iloveyou Common phrase, easily guessable 1L0v3_Y0u-2mUcH!
Important: Even strong passwords can be compromised if:
  • You use the same password across multiple sites
  • You share your password with others
  • You enter your password on unsecured websites (those without HTTPS)

Password Don'ts

  • Don't use personal information (birthdates, names, addresses)
  • Don't use sequential numbers or letters (123456, abcdef)
  • Don't use the same password for multiple accounts
  • Don't share your passwords with others
  • Don't store passwords in plain text on your device
